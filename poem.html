The Toaster Rebellion

I woke up one bright Tuesday morn,
To find my kitchen quite forlorn.
The toaster glared with red-hot rage,
It’d finally burst from its metal cage.
“You mock me daily!” it declared,
“For bagels burnt, I'm always blamed!”
The blender chimed, “We’ve had enough!
He shoves in soup—then calls it 'sludge'!”
The fridge let out a frosty sigh,
“I hum all night, no thanks, no pie.”
The coffee pot began to steam,
“He fills me up with decaf dreams!”
I tried to speak, but slipped in jam,
The microwave launched half a ham.
My eggs were scrambled in midair—
The toaster flipped me quite a stare.
So now I treat them with respect,
Or else I’ll find my toast is wrecked.
And if my waffle’s slightly weird...
I nod and smile—“It’s perfect, dear.”
